Who Am I?

Hi! My name's Marcus and I'm a scientist that really likes seaweed!


I graduated from the University of California, Los Angeles in 2022 with a B.Sc. in Ecology, Evolution, & Behavior and will be pursuing a M.Sc. in Earth & Planetary Sciences at McGill University. I'm currently co-advised by Dr. Nagissa Mahmoudi and Dr. Rowan Barrett.


Aside from science (or science-adjacent things), I am an ex-ski instructor from Lake Louise; a former students events planner; an amateur web developer (I built this from scratch! - I'm too cheap to pay for squarespace), an ex-barback, an aquarist/aquaculturist, and love teaching others about marine science! I'm very much a jack of all trades, master of none and am always looking for opportunities to flex my non-science muscles once in a while.

Things That Interest Me

I've worked in a number of different environments with a a variety of species. From the secondary forests in Madagascar; the Central Pacific Ocean in French Polynesia; the Eastern Pacific in Southern California; to (hopefully!) the Atlantic in Eastern Canada, I've worked on herpetofauna (reptiles/amphibians); lepidopterans (butterflies/moths); stony corals; estuarine fish; and most importantly— macroalgae!


Besides culturing seaweeds, I'm most familiar with the fate of nitrogen and sulfur in marine environments (primarily in anaerobic sediments) and geospatial information systems (GIS) as it pertains to California geomorphology. I have some background of stony coral larval proteomics, behavioral ecology, and general molecular biology. For past work, please refer to the descriptions in the CV - I go much more in depth there!


Currently, I work on the associated surface microbiome of giant kelp sporophytes from a farm in Santa Barbara and compare differences in the microbial community structure across different measured traits. I aim to take this work with me to McGill University to further examine the microbiome of seaweeds local to the Atlantic such as sugar kelp (Saccharina latissma) winged kelp, (Alaria esculenta), or dulse (Palmaria palmata).

Life Experiences

FALL 2023

McGill University (M.Sc.)

Nagissa Mahmoudi & Rowan Barrett Lab

As an recipient of the McCall MacBain Scholars Finalist Award ($20,000 CAD), I am pursuing an M.Sc. in the Earth & Planerary Sciences department. I'll be advised by Nagissa Mahmoudi, a geomicrobiologist, and Rowan Barrett, a bioinformatician, and (hopefully) working on seaweed microbiomes in changing ocean conditions. Rising temperatures, oil spills, and increased nutrient runoff all threaten the biodiversity of our coastlines, although we have some idea of how macroalgae are affected at large, there is little known about the specific changes in their associated microbiota.

NOW - JUL 2022

University of Southern California

Sergey Nuzhdin Lab - Lab Manager

With Dr. Sergey Nuzhdin, I acted as the lab manager for both his molecular biology lab and off-campus aquaculture facility. I was responsible for general lab inventory, administration, and safety while managing kelp-specific projects. During my time, I managed one of the first germplasm collections ("seed bank") for Macrocystis pyrifera in Southern California; temporarily managed the kelp elevator project investigating experimental cycling of giant kelp from nutrient-rich deep water to photic surface as an offshore farm proof of concept; selectively bred then grew potentially sporeless (non-reproductive) cultivars of giant kelp on-land; and analyzed the microbiome of giant kelp sporophytes from a farm in Santa Barbara. With our aquaculture facility housed at AltaSea, I participated in education programs (85+ students and guests) and interviews on our work. During my time at USC, I was fortunate to be able to mentor five paid undergraduate researchers (who are all super sick!).

Mentees: Omar Omar (Work-Study), Cate Baroni (Work-Study), Antonella Pardo-Figueroa (Work-Study), Tamara Margaryan (Summer Undergraduate Research Fund), Dylan Hadfield (USDA Fellow) - Thank you all for your hard work!

JUN 2022 - JAN 2020

Tina Treude Oceanography Lab - Research Assistant

In Dr. Tina Treude's lab, I worked primarily with David Yousavich, Ph.D.(c), and helped study the fate of nitrate and sulfur compounds in the Salton Sea, an anoxic lake, while acting as a technician in caring for general lab maintenance. JUN 2022 - AUG 2019

David K. Jacobs Lab - Undergraduate Researcher

For several years, I worked with Dr. David Jacobs on a number of different projects— primarily on coastal geomorphology and some geochemistry. I classified geomorphological processes and features of coastal estuaries, lagoons, and watersheds in California from aerial imagery and digital elevation models. This work was then applied to understanding the geohydrology of rainwater in Malibu, CA and the distribution of an endangered tidewater goby. Lastly, I worked on a collaborative project between Dr. Jacobs and Dr. Kevin Mckeegan on exploring Secondary Ion Mass Spectrometry (SIMS) on fish otoliths as environmental geochemical markers of lagoon conditions through time.

SEP 2021 - JUN 2021

Boston College

Wang Stable Isotope Biogeochemistry Lab - Summer Researcher

Over a summer, I worked with Dr. Xingchen (Tony) Wang to analyze nitrogen, carbon, and oxygen isotopes in seawater samples and coral tissue samples. Through the seawater samples, we could estimate how much of the nitrogen observed was anthropogenic runoff versus natural sources. In the stony corals, we were exploring if there was an increase in heavier nitrogen isotopes in aposymbiotic (non-photosynthesizing) corals as photosynthetic corals would internally cycle their nitrogen. I also acted as the on-site technician for the lab's isotope ratio mass spectrometer (IRMS) with a modified Gas Bench (for N2O analyses) and Elemental Analyzer (EA).
